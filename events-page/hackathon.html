Introduction



Hackathon is an event conducted with the idea to bring people from different walks of life, possessing different sets of skills, to work together and solve some real-world problems using their attained skills. The most important part of any hackathon is team building and utilization of the resources available may it be in the form of knowledge, expertise, or material. Hackathon will make the teams work at their full potential. Hackathon provides an environment where all teams work under time and resource constraints. This helps bring out the best results.

Flow of Event


1. The event will commence on 6/04/2022, at 6.00 Pm, with the formation of teams and allotment of chosen problem statements, which is to be conducted online.
2. The team will be given ample time of 2 days to build the software and hardware.
3. All the teams are required to gather at the specified labs to work on the problem and mentor walkthrough.
4. The participants will be free to work at the specified labs in the time given.
5. The teams are allowed to work in the labs on 8/04/2022 too.
6. On 8/04/2022, the teams will be called for the presentation of their solution to
the problem statements. All teams must gather whenever asked to.

Note: Participants are required to bring their own laptops and other devices (if required).

Who can Participate?


Students from all branches of engineering can participate in the event. The event is open for all engineering undergraduates, irrespective of college or university. Students with the knowledge of computer coding and problem-solving skills can participate.

Rules and Regulations


1. Registering for the hackathon is entirely free of charge.
2. Each team must be given a name as chosen by its members. The team’s name must be neutral in nature and must not be too flashy. Team size should be of 3 - 5 members
3. Participating teams are required to provide correct information about each and every team member during the online application. Any ambiguous entry will not be considered.
4. Eligible Submissions will be evaluated by a panel of judges selected by the Administrator (the “Judges”). Judging may take place in one or more rounds with one or more panels of Judges, at the discretion of the Administrator.
5. Judges are not required to test the Application and may choose to judge based solely on the text description, images, and video provided in the Submission.
6. Administrators reserve the right in their sole discretion to disqualify any individual or Maker it finds to be actually or presenting the appearance of tampering with the entry process or the operation of the Hackathon or to be acting in violation of these Official Rules or in a manner that is inappropriate, not in the best interests of this Hackathon, or a violation of any applicable law or regulation.
7. You will be bound by and comply with these Official Rules and the decisions of the Administrator, and/or the Hackathon Judges which are binding and final in all matters relating to the Hackathon.
8. Any kind of cheating and plagiarism will lead to disqualification of the team and it will be barred to participate in further events.
9. Participating teams are required to coordinate with the management team of IIC for the smooth execution of the event.
10. Students are required to bring their own laptops and other devices (if required).

PS7 Background: The world’s natural resources are already depleting, and people are starting to feel the repercussions of a wasteful lifestyle. As such, energy conservation is vital in today’s world. Not only does it help households save money, but it also encourages consumers to be more conscious about preserving natural resources. IoT has made homes smarter, so its application in energy conservation is more accessible. Smart appliances can inform homeowners of real-time consumption so that they can monitor their energy usage. Reports can easily be generated or extracted, allowing homeowners to monitor their daily, weekly, and monthly energy consumption accurately.
In addition, sensors can be installed so that when owners leave the house, appliances will automatically turn off to conserve energy. Summary: In order to conserve the energy, we need to keep a check on all the type of resources that are being used in our day-to-day life. With IoT it becomes easy to track the resources we are using and we can monitor and keep a check on. Reports generated from such monitoring system can help in conserving natural resources and reduce unnecessary wastage if any. Objective: We would like you to develop a mobile/web app system using IoT to make energy conservation easy. Also provide reports on usage of all the resources in day-to-day life and smart automation methods to reduce the amounts of any resource we use.		 Software

JUDGING CRITERIA:

Technology and Design
Difficulty of Task (1-5 points) X Execution (1-5 points)
Judge's Favorite: Each Technical Judge may award an additional five points for their favorite use of technology.
Maximum of 30 points from each technical judge.

Community Impact
Research and Analysis (1-5 points) X Readability (1-5 points)
Judge's Favorite: Each Community Impact Judge may award an additional five points for their favorite demonstration of community impact.
Maximum of 30 points from each Community Impact Judge.

Presentation
Information Communicated (1-5 points) X Quality of Delivery (1-5 points)
Judge's Favorite: Each Presentation Judge may award an additional five points for their favorite presentation
Maximum of 30 points from each presentation judge

Budget/Scope of Work
Breakdown of hypothetical budget and scope of work report (1-5 points)
Judge's Favorite: Each Presentation Judge may award an additional five points for their favorite presentation
Maximum of 30 points from each presentation judge

Total Team Score
The maximum score possible for each category will be the same.
Total Score = Technology + Community Impact + Presentation + Budget/Scope of Work

Penalties: Score of ZERO if not submitted on time or if started before event kickoff
